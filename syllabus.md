<h2>Logic Circuit Design (LCD):</h2>
<h1>“A New Golden Age for Computer Architecture: Domain-Specific Hardware/Software Co-Design,</h1><h1> Enhanced Security, Open Instruction Sets, and Agile Chip Development”</h1>
<h1>By John L. Hennessy and David A. Patterson</h1>
<h1>Logic circuit design course aims to make you familiar with the digital system concepts,</h1> <h1>digital storages, digital system modeling, and digital system design. This course is</h1><h1>one of the main courses in bachelor program in top universities around the world, such</h1><h1>as MIT, Berkley, Stanford, ETH Zurich, USC, Caltech, British Columbia, EPFL, and etc.</h1>
<h2>lesson results:</h2>
<h1> Students who successfully complete this course will have a good insight into:</h1>
<h1> 1- Fundamental understanding of digital systems and circuits</h1>
<h1>2- Design of digital systems</h1> 
<h1>3- Analysis of digital systems</h1> 
<h1>4- Digital modeling of digital systems</h1> 
<h2>list of topics:</h2>
<h1>1-Basic principles and concepts:</h1> 
<h1> History of digital systems</h1>
<h1>The application of digital systems in the present world</h1> 
<h1>The foundations of digital systems and their differences with analog systems</h1> 
<h1> Overview of digital circuits based on MOS transistors</h1>
<h1> 2-Numerical systems:</h1>
<h1> Number theory and their representation</h1>
<h1>Computing in digital systems</h1> 
<h1> Concepts of Carry and Overflow</h1>
<h1>BCD display systems</h1> 
<h1>3-Algebra Bull:</h1> 
<h1> The principles of Boolean algebra</h1>
<h1>Logical functions, operators and gates</h1> 
<h1> Boolean algebraic relationships</h1>
<h1>Canonical, Minterm, Maxterm display and standard forms display functions logical</h1> 
<h1>VHDL or Verilog hardware description language training at the structural level</h1> 
<h1>4-Analysis and design of hybrid logic systems:</h1> 
<h1>Techniques for simplifying composite circuits with Boolean algebra</h1> 
<h1> Optimization of hybrid circuits with Carnot table and Quine-McCallowski algorithm and The concept of care does not matter  </h1>
<h1>Glitch and Hazard, Race Concept</h1> 
<h1>Types of implementation of two-stage circuits</h1> 
<h1> Delay concept</h1>
<h1>Encoders, decoders, multipliers, antiparticulators, weekly and applicationsThey are particularly useful as a building block in the design of logic circuits</h1>  
<h1> Design with Universal Gates (Universal)</h1>
<h1>Distributed, Comparative, Collective Circuit Predictor Circuits</h1> 
<h1>The concept of high impedance and the use of three-state buffers to create high impedance, circuit withOpen collector gates, wire logic, use of resistors as pull-up and pull-down</h1> 
<h1>(PAL, PLA, FPGA) Programmable</h1> 
<h1> Introduction of standard composite chips</h1>
<h1>5-Analyzing and designing sequential logic systems</h1> 
<h1> Introducing memory elements, latches and flippops</h1>
<h1>Delay of Memory Elements Release, Startup and Retention Time Concepts, Inputs Synchronous and Asynchronous</h1> 
<h1> Analysis of sequential circuits, excitation table, mode diagram, mode table</h1>
<h1> FSM design steps, Mealy and Moore models and their differences</h1>
<h1> Design of sequential circuits with different types of flipflops</h1>
<h1>Universal counters, registers and shifters and registers</h1> 
<h1>Introducing standard sequential chips</h1> 
<h1> 6-The basics of designing asynchronous circuits</h1>
